mID

The future of medical services

mID is a combination of a chip and a software which is implanted in ones body that transmits data like Temperature, Blood Pressure, Glucose levels and an ID which is linked to a central database of previous medical records of a person

Healthcare > Product Design

THE CHALLENGE

New generation - more population - the more relevant is the cause of creating an central database on medical history for each individuals. The real struggle were those that were in immediate life threatening situations and old aged people.

We embarked on a journey to identify potential solutions to solve this problem in order to increase efficiency of doctors and hospital staff.

Design Process

DISCOVER

When patients are older, obtaining a good history—including information on social circumstances and lifestyle in addition to medical and family history—is crucial to good health care.

We identified all adult patients who underwent emergency surgery between January 2012 and October 2014 at a single tertiary care centre Of 15,160 patients, 2820 (18.6%) experienced a delay. The mortality rates were 4.9% (138/2820) for those with delay.

Delayed operating room access for emergency surgery was associated with increased risk of in-hospital mortality, longer length of stay and higher costs. System issues appeared to underlie most delays.

Source - https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5505757/

DEFINE

Through various brainstorming exercises, we dabbled with product concepts spanning across all technology, After prototyping, we arrived at our final idea by marrying different elements from various ideas into one concept - a implantable chip with cloud based software.

SOLUTION

mID is a combination of a chip and a software made in focused mainly for elderly people for continous monitoring of Blood pressure, Temperature, Glucose levels, Respiratory systems and to access past medical records with ease.

We focused on user’s convenience in mind that it enhances the usability for elderly people and accessing past medical histories for Doctors.

HOW?

Mobile phones communicate by transmitting radio waves through a network of fixed antennas called base stations. Radiofrequency waves are electromagnetic fields, and unlike ionizing radiation such as X-rays or gamma rays, can neither break chemical bonds nor cause ionization in the human body.
